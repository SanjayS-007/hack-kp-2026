## 3. Revenue Model Recommendation

Govtech monetizes three ways; AEGIS should blend them, anchored on **platform licensing** (like Cellebrite/Palantir) rather than pure per-seat (which caps a sovereign, air-gapped deployment).

| Model | How it works | Fit for AEGIS | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Per-seat SaaS** | Annual license per examiner | Secondary | Cellebrite UFED **~$5k–12k/seat/yr** ([procurement records]) |
| **Per-case / consumption** | Fee per device or case processed | Add-on / cloud burst | Aligns with backlog pain |
| **Platform / enterprise license** ✅ | Site license per agency + modules + support | **Primary** — matches air-gapped on-prem | Cellebrite ARR model ($480.8M ARR, 21% YoY); Palantir enterprise deals |

**Recommended packaging — 3 tiers (annual, recurring):**
- **Cyber Cell (pilot):** ~$150k–300k/yr — core triage + report + support (single unit).
- **State / Central Agency:** ~$750k–1.5M/yr — full fusion, graph, deepfake shield, multi-seat, CCTNS integration.
- **National platform (I4C-scale):** ~$3–8M/yr — cross-case network, all modules, SLA, on-prem GPU.

**Blended target ACV ≈ $500k–1M per agency**, sold through **India's GeM (Government e-Marketplace)** — the mandated, transparent route MHA already uses for I4C IT/software procurement ([GeM](https://bidplus.gem.gov.in/)). This mirrors Cellebrite's **ARR-led model** (recurring > 80% of revenue) rather than one-time perpetual licenses.

## 5. Five Moats

| # | Moat | Mechanism | Why it compounds |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Data-network effects** | Hash + wallet + entity nodes are *global*; every new case enriches the cross-case knowledge graph (GNN link-prediction) | Each agency added makes syndicate discovery better for all — Flock/Chainalysis-style network value ($7.5B / $8.6B valuations built on this) |
| 2 | **Regulatory / court moat** | BSA 2023 §63 auto-certificates + SHA-256 chain of custody + HERAM/ECS hallucination-scoring = evidence that survives cross-examination | Court-validated output is *reference-able precedent*; competitors must re-earn admissibility jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction |
| 3 | **Switching costs** | Air-gapped on-prem deployment, warrant-scoped RBAC, immutable audit logs, deep CCTNS/Dial-112/VAAHAN integration | Rip-and-replace risks chain-of-custody continuity on live cases — agencies do not churn mid-investigation |
| 4 | **Sovereign air-gap** | Local LLMs, zero data egress, India-resident | Regulatory + procurement requirement that US-cloud incumbents (Cellebrite, Palantir) structurally cannot match for sensitive jurisdictions |
| 5 | **Wellbeing certification** | Blur-by-default, ~91% exposure reduction; instrumentable PTSD-mitigation KPI | An unclaimed differentiation axis; becomes a *procurement checkbox* once one agency mandates it, locking out non-compliant vendors |

## 6. Impact Economics — the social ROI underneath the revenue

- **Investigator attrition/PTSD:** 10–40% of CSAM investigators show significant PTSD/secondary-trauma symptoms. Replacing a trained investigator costs **~50–200% of salary (~$75k–150k)**; annual mental-health support runs **$2k–10k+/person** ([Guardian, 2021](https://www.theguardian.com/society/2021/nov/16/detectives-investigating-child-abuse-suffer-vicarious-trauma-report-finds)). AEGIS's ~91% exposure reduction directly attacks this cost.
- **Backlog cost:** 25,000+ devices queued in UK forces alone, delays of **6–18 months** — every month is investigative decay and a child left at risk. AEGIS compresses triage to **hours**.
- **Value of early rescue:** CDC estimates the **lifetime cost of a single child-abuse survivor at ~$210k–250k**; child-protection interventions return **~$4–6 per $1 invested** ([CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/), [NSPCC SROI](https://learning.nspcc.org.uk/research-resources/2016/social-return-on-investment-evidence-review)). A platform that surfaces even a handful of additional active-abuse rescues per agency per year pays for itself many times over in avoided lifetime social cost.

**Framing:** AEGIS is not a cost center — it is a **cost-avoidance and capacity-multiplier** engine. One prevented investigator burnout ≈ one license; one early rescue ≈ ~$250k avoided lifetime cost.
