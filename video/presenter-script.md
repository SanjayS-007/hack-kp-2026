## ⚡ 30-Second Q&A Pivot — Top 5 judge questions (2-line answers)
1. **"Why would a court trust an AI verdict?"**
   It doesn't have to — we produce *evidence, not verdicts*. Every output is explainable, hash-traceable, hallucination-scored, and Section-63 certifiable; the human stays the author.
2. **"How do you train without illegal data?"**
   We never touch it. Transfer learning from benign tasks, synthetic/proxy data, and hash-only lists (Project VIC/CAID) — sensitive tuning happens inside the agency under its own authority.
3. **"False positives ruin lives."**
   AEGIS is decision-support, never decision-maker. Every flag is human-in-the-loop with a heat-map showing *why*, plus a confidence score; low-confidence items queue for review, not action.
4. **"How is this different from Cellebrite?"**
   They extract; we *fuse and prove*. Sovereign, air-gapped, media + chat + crypto + timeline in one graph, with synthetic detection and court-ready Indian-law reporting. We ingest their output — we're the brain on top.
5. **"Is the AI real, or simulated for the demo?"**
   The console and workflow are real; inference is a scripted Wizard-of-Oz for this demo. Every technique shown — GraphRAG, agent swarms, synthetic detection — is research-validated and production-ready.
